Lessons Learnt

The main lessons learnt by each Council is outlined below.

  • Involve the right people with the right skills.
  • Business continuity plan needs to include pandemic risk.
  • Look after your people.
  • Early transparent engagement and communication with feedback loop.
  • Structure with key relationships and communication with agencies.
  • Pandemic sub-plan in the disaster management plan.
  • Communication management must be well done.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help when needed.
  • Adaptability is critical specially for small council.
  • Trust was really important (private information of others) – external and internal (e.g. job security).
  • Empathy for others (especially neighbouring councils) with critical circumstances.
  • Include pandemic in disaster management plan and business continuity plan.
  • Lead by example.
  • Immediacy of response and reprioritisation of work.
  • Celebrate successes.
  • Value in disaster management and business continuity planning and testing, including out of the box thinking.
  • Importance in flexibility and resilience of Information Technology systems.
  • Support, trust and flexibility for staff is critical to manage sometimes unknown situations.
  • Regularly review and exercise the business continuity plan.
  • Ensure decisions and actions are supported by relevant risk assessment.
  • Communication at all levels is critical.
